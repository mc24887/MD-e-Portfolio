Research Methods & Professional Practice In Progress

Module Overview

This e-portfolio documents my progress across Units 1–12 in Research Methods & Professional Practice. It includes ethical analyses, collaborative discussion work, a literature review (Unit 7, summative), proposal preparation, statistical activities, and visual analysis. Each unit entry highlights key submissions, evidence, reflection, and skills developed. Units 9–12 are shown with Pending where work has not yet been submitted.

Unit 1 – Scientific Investigation & Ethics

Key Submissions
  • Initial Forum Post – ACM Case Study (Inadequate Security Measures): Applied ACM 1.2 (avoid harm) and 2.9 (secure systems) to a release with known vulnerabilities; compared with BCS public interest and integrity duties; noted potential DPA 2018 implications.
Reflection

I learned to translate ethical codes into practical decisions (e.g., risk escalation, documentation, secure defaults) rather than treating them as abstract principles.

Skills Developed
  • Ethical reasoning in computing
  • Application of ACM/BCS codes
  • Structured argumentation

Unit 2 – Research Questions, Literature Review & Proposal

Key Submissions
  • Literature Review Outline: Topic: Generative AI Risks in Software Security and Email Workflows; RQs grouped (offensive, defensive, governance); inclusion/exclusion and databases (IEEE, ACM, Scopus).
  • Method: Systematic approach (Dawson, 2015) and PRISMA-style screening plan.
Reflection

Defining tight RQs and scope was crucial; inclusion/exclusion rules prevent drift and ensure a credible review for Unit 7.

Skills Developed
  • RQ formulation
  • Systematic search design
  • Screening discipline

Unit 3 – Methodology & Research Methods

Key Submissions
  • Method/Design Map: Qualitative, quantitative, mixed methods; exploratory vs descriptive design; primary vs secondary data choices linked to my project.
Reflection

Matching methods to RQs (not the reverse) improved rigour. Early consideration of validity, reliability, and generalisability reduces later rework.

Skills Developed
  • Method selection
  • Design rationale
  • Risk/assumption awareness

Unit 4 – Case Studies, Focus Groups & Observation

Key Submissions
  • Methods Overview: Case studies (depth, limited generalisability), focus groups (6–10 participants with common background), quantitative/qualitative observation (objective measures vs naturalistic behaviour).
  • Applied Ethics Scenario (Privacy): Access control, consent, and data minimisation considerations for contacting individuals via records data.
Reflection

No single method is perfect. Combining methods (e.g., observation + focus group) improves triangulation; consent and governance remain central.

Skills Developed
  • Method comparison
  • Ethical analysis in data collection
  • Triangulation planning

Unit 5 – Interviews, Surveys & Questionnaire Design

Data Collection Update

Mixed-methods plan for my project: survey (quantitative breadth) + semi-structured interviews (qualitative depth). Piloting, transparent consent, and data minimisation embedded.

Wiki – Questionnaire Design
  • Clear, unbiased wording; one construct per item.
  • Direct alignment with objectives; pilot before deployment.
  • Ethics: purpose limitation, consent, retention/deletion.
Reflective Activity 2 – Inappropriate Use of Surveys

Analysis of Cambridge Analytica (2018): purpose drift, opaque permissions, and misuse of data for targeting. Impacts: ethical (consent), social (trust/democracy), legal (GDPR/DPA), professional (ACM/BCS duties). Commitments: proportional data, explicit consent, DPIA where appropriate.

Skills Developed
  • Instrument design
  • Survey ethics & governance
  • Mixed-methods integration
